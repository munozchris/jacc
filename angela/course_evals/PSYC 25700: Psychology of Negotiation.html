What, if anything, what would you change about this course and why?

  • Make the negotiations count, it should be like 5% of the grade (so not enough to stress people out), but enough to get people to put in effort for the exercises in class.
  • I would change the grading system to somehow incorporate how well people do on the negotiations themselves, as the writing assignments were not very useful for the class.
  • More writing assignments, worth a smaller percentage of the grade each. More direction on the final paper
  • I think i would benefit as a psych major from a more in-depth consideration of negotiation issues psychologically. The content aims to engage both psych majors and non-psych majors, but the end result is a lot of repetition for psych majors in the course of concepts given all the other electives concerned with behavioral economic-type topics.
  • I thought this course was kind of boring...I felt I got more out of decision making - but maybe that's just me? I didn't like the three hour seminar format at all. It made the second half very slow and painful as most of us would be getting very sleep around 3 pm (the class is from 1:30-4:20). I also felt like the readings from last quarter were more riveting. I also felt the final paper was not a good measure of our knowledge. I got a good grade, and all, so that's not why I am complaining - it just felt like an extended literary review/summary. I didn't feel like I adequately expressed any new thought.
  • Really nothing
  • Overall I think this course was really well-designed. The format, with negotiation exercises, the final paper, mini response papers, and mini exercises all seemed to have a purpose and presented a good variety of things we could be graded on. Short answer: wouldn't change a thing.
  • Meet more than once a week.
  • The deadlines on the syllabus were planned out in an odd fashion - the last writing response was due well before the end of the class. The final was due during 10th week, even though a finals week deadline would have been a lot better for most people. And, after all, we have a reading period so we can work on finals - making a final due before reading period seems odd. Also, it wasn't clear to what extent the TA's graded the assignments equally strictly - in a class like this, where there were no TA sessions, perhaps TA's could trade off whose assignments they grade each week so that each student has an equal number of grades given to him/her by each TA? I think this would allow for a fairer grading scheme. Lastly, the negotiation prep sheets were a MAJOR part of my prep for this class, and yet they were only 7% of my grade. Yet, the writing responses, of which there were only 4, were 35%. I think the prep sheets should be weighed more within the grading rubric and perhaps assigned some sort of incomplete/partial completion/completion points, if not an actual grade (there were many of them and they took a significant amount of time).
  • I would bring in more guest lecturers, and eliminate the Ultimatum Game simulation

  • Practical and theoretical consideration of an important issue (negotiation) :)
