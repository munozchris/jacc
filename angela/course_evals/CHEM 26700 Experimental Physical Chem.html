What were the instructor's weak points?

  • Because he is so intelligent and knows so much about the material he is teaching, Prof Engel often went way too fast through complicated material and assumed we had an understanding that we didn't. He seemed to go very quickly through important material while spending a lot of time covering less important material.
  • Lectures were unclear, and random. Would be nice for there to be a specific topic covered. Often used technical language that few people understood.
  • He often spent a lot of time talking about his own work and while it was interesting, I'm not sure how relevant it was some of the time.
  • While the review of the previous lecture in the beginning of each lecture is good in theory, in practice it meant we only progressed about 40 minutes forward in material each lecture. Especially since we weren't really learning very intense concepts, it was probably overkill.
  • He often spoke too quickly and skipped over the more basic information that would have been helpful in terms of understanding the harder material.
  • Just a couple suggestions for improvement: 1. Include many more example problems in the lectures. It was hard to learn the material by going directly from abstract theory to very specific (yet still mostly qualitative) applications. 2. Though I'm sure Prof. Engel didn't mean this, his discussion of the Flint water crisis in terms of children's IQ distribution came off as a bit insensitive. I might suggest steering clear of that in the future.

  • He sometimes repeats things that were in previous lectures, but rarely.
