College Course Evaluations

The University of Chicago

TAPS 10200: Acting Fundamentals

Section 01 - Winter 2016

Instructor(s): Stalling, Vanessa
Number Enrolled: 15
Number of Responses: 4

Evaluation Comments

What were the instructor's strengths? Weaknesses?

  • She didn't seem very invested in students.
  • Vanessa has an excellent way of motivating the class and making people shed their inhibitions. She provided very useful, clear feedback on our acted scenes in class. I also really liked how she took lots of time analyzing each little piece of a dialogue or play, making us try to see it from all kinds of different angles. I do wish we had gone into a little more depth with our acting, and had had less straight-cut expectations, because I feel like the framework we were taught to follow (of Gedons and essential actions), while it really helped us stay authentic and in character, also felt slightly restrictive since it was harder to incorporate complex or conflicting emotions, states of mind that were self-absorbed, or low stakes interactions between characters. However I suppose this would have been quite hard to do given that we were all newcomers to the acting field. Overall - this was a great class, very enjoyable, and Vanessa is a great person and teacher.
  • Prof. Stalling was engaging and provided a lot of good feedback on all of the scenes and written assignments we did.
  • Vanessa is an extremely kind and knowledgable instructor. She knows her acting, and she is very patient with students. I highly recommend her.

What, if anything, what would you change about this course and why?

  • It's fine the way it is. Can't expect too much from art core.
  • More flexible frameworks (see above)
  • I wish we analyzed some more scenes and did some more activities. Other than that, I thought the course was great.
  • Maybe more acting games at the beginning to get a feel for being onstage? This is a minor critique.

  • Prior acting experience would have been cool.

  • N/A
  • The short plays - I wish we had read more through the quarter.
  • Kirk was somewhat useful, but could have been more straightforward.
  • The Space Between, Mud

Which least?

  • Kirk
  • Kirk was the main text we used. It was okay, but I am glad we did not spend a great deal of time on it.

How productive was class discussion?

  • Sometimes we summarized the reading in a very methodical way, other times we came up with synonyms for words. Discussion was always dull and it was clear that the only thing anyone had on the line was their participation grade.
  • Mostly very productive. Occasionally people had to be coaxed to talk more, but in general there was a high level of enthusiasm towards discussion.
  • Discussion about the Kirk readings felt a bit flat. However, the table work we did for our final scenes was useful and insightful.
  • Class discussion was very productive. In a class this fun, morale is always pretty high and discussion is helpful.

How has this course contributed to your education?

  • Some of the stuff about focusing on being observed helps me look normal in everyday situations. I also feel a bit more confident in my ability to carry myself since I started acting and thinking about what motivates me in scenes.
  • Solid foundational intro to acting!
  • I think I better understand how to approach acting. I also think the course made me a better reader. In analyzing different scenes, I got to practice reading carefully to determine the goals of each character and to look beyond the surface level of the text.
  • I'm a better and more confident actor and public speaker.
