Explain:

  • The text was the worst organized language text I have ever used. Greek Today was my nemesis. Not having vocabulary lists with definitions in the book at the beginning of every chapter was very difficult, and I felt like all of the pronouns should have been presented at the same time. There were a lot of grammatical concepts that the book assumed we had knowledge of without ever laying them out.
  • Chrysanthi did a great job explaining all of the material in the text; although at times she deviated from the textbook material. Definitely not a bad thing, though
  • She gave us handouts fairly frequently when the text book lacked adequate explanation. The text book is terrible.
  • The regular textbook was insufficient for some of the important topics we covered so Chrysanthi supplemented the lessons with external exercises. This generally worked well in class, but it was rather difficult when it came to homework since all of the vocabulary from the supplementary documents was entirely different from the vocabulary we covered from the textbook. This resulted in a lot of hours spent just looking up external vocabulary that we did not use much afterward. I know that she prefers other textbooks, so I think it would be ideal for future courses to use the book(s) that she knows to be best.
  • Chrysanthi did a great job of using the text for as much as it was useful for and then using other handouts as supplement when the book was not helpful enough. I didn't mind the book as much as other people, I still used it a lot, but it could be better.

  • Very applicable vocab which one might be able to use within a couple weeks of the course

  • The vocabulary in the textbook was useful for basic communication and we used those terms frequently in class, but the vocabulary required for the external exercises that she assigned was often much less useful for everyday circumstances.
  • I think it was mostly useful. There were some more obscure words that we learned before more basic ones at times, which I thought was strange but I think it was to fit with the theme of the chapter. Overall, when you don't know any Greek any Greek vocabulary is useful!

Describe the format of the class (lecture, drills, etc). Was this format productive? Please be specific.

  • Speaking in class, quizzes,
  • The format of the course was a mix of exercises and dialogues from the book, supplemented by Chrysanthi's general knowledge.
  • discussion based class with numerous grammar lectures, discussions, activities
  • Lecture and practicing grammar and writing through homework, it was productive, but learning on our own from the text book when we needed to was difficult.
  • The class was a productive mix of lecture, drills, and group exercises, but I feel like we could have benefited from more time practicing drills out loud as a whole class so that we could learn more from each other's mistakes.
