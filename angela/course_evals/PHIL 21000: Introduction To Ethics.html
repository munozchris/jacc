The University of Chicago

PHIL 21000: Introduction To Ethics

Instructor(s): Callard, Benjamin; Pocius, Skomantas
Identical Courses: HIPS 21000, FNDL 23107

Evaluation Comments

What were the instructor's strengths? Weaknesses?

  • Its a Callard class, so you expect little and get less. I'm not sure what part of class is worse, his 'lectures' or having to listen to all of the bleeding hearts in the class argue with feelings and not facts. But, if there is to be something admirable about class was its consistency, for each session never failed to subtract from the sum of human knowledge.
  • Ben was really good at directing discussion despite the size of the class, and was really good at directing conversations that were not just speaking to him, instead of other students. However, since it was a large class, there was still a good amount of lecture, which he presented in an interesting but informative way. Sometimes he could dwell too long on specific subjects, but he was always clear about when he would go over specific parts of the material. He was so funny and awesome, such a great teacher, and was really enthusiastic about the material and the things his students had to say!!
  • The question of whether I would want to take another class from Ben is a tricky one. On one hand, I would leave class lamenting that the aggregate level of wisdom of the human race had seemingly been detracted from in a meaningful way in the preceding hour and twenty minutes. On the other, Ben structured the course in such a way that did not require attendance at lecture, and thus my exposure to such radical and illuminating ideas as the ambiguity of vegetable capacity for pain was minimized. That said, Ben is a genuinely nice guy.
  • Professor Callard is an impressive teacher. Despite the fact that the students in this class were the dumbest bunch I've seen in my years at this school, Professor Callard was able to weave our comments into a coherent narrative. He was able to find serious philosophical positions in what sounded to me like students' aimless rambling and show the upsides and downsides of that view. I came out of every class with not just new knowledge but a newfound curiosity. Only weakness is that he could have been more demanding.
  • Ben is great! His lectures are really illuminating and he always has good responses to student questions/comments
  • Professor Callard explained concepts well and generally seemed like an nice guy.
  • Ben is great!! He's so so nice and really loves philosophy. He always has a smile during lecture and gets excited when we get something right or contribute. Made me want to participate a lot more
  • Dr. Callard is excellent. He is an engaging lecturer and a brilliant thinker. He listens thoughtfully to student input and debates with students (always kindly) to challenge our thinking.
  • Lively person and good lecturer. He tried to get a lot of class participation and that was good, but having participation be so much of your grade at the end makes no sense when the class is over 50 people.
  • Ben is amazing. He is absolutely brilliant and he loves teaching, and it really shows during class. He is a joy to learn from.
  • Professor Callard is wonderful. So knowledgable in the field of ethics. He clearly enjoys the act of teaching and engaging students. He is liberal with his acknowledgment of potential flaws in his own theories and invites students to consider that different paths. I have never had a professor like him who has convinced me that he is genuinely pursuing the same path of inquiry (if heading in different directions) as me--although he is quite the expert, he is humble in his role as an eternal learner. Finally, he is wonderful about responding to emails/providing students with additional resources to explore areas of curiosity.
  • Prof. Callard was exceptional, he assigns managable reading and gets us to think about things in new ways, he hopes to learn from us as we learn from him and he is able to do this in a big 60 person class.
