The University of Chicago

INST 23102: Contemporary Global Issues-2

Section 01 - Winter 2016

Instructor(s): Jasarevic, Larisa
Identical Courses: GLST 23102
Number Enrolled: 10
Number of Responses: 4

Evaluation Comments

What were the instructor's strengths? Weaknesses?

  • She seemed to care a lot about the topics that interested her but if it was not something that she was passionate about then she disregarded it is unimportant. She had favorites in the class and it was obvious to everyone. If a student was on her bad side then nothing they did was correct and meeting with her to receive constructive advice was often not helpful. Often times people will drop the Global Studies major after Contemporary Global Issues 1 because of this professor being disorganized and not teaching anything of value. My experience in part 2 of the course was so overwhelmingly negative that I wish I would have done the same.
  • Very mean Harsh grading Wants everything in her specific way And then when you follow her instructions it's still wrong
  • Professor Jasarevic clearly cares about the subject matter but she has a hard time translating that into cohesive class discussions. The demands of the class were very unclear so even if discussion went well, it didn't have anything to do with the assignments and the assignments were graded rather unfairly. It seems like if she likes you, you'll get a better grade. If not, too bad. There was also a tendency to focus a lot on certain keywords when the actual connection of those keywords to the more global aspects wasn't really clear. In general, I could tell that Larisa tried to teach us but it usually just ended up in mutual confusion.
