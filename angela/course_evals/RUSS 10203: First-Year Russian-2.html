Describe the format of the class (lecture, drills, etc). Was this format productive? Please be specific.

  • Lecture and drills are pretty interchangeable. At the beginning of the course, there was more pair-work than towards the end (I think, because we're running out of time). I think the pairwise practice of concepts is really integral, even if it takes longer. In the last ~3 or so weeks we've transitioned into mostly doing class-wide call-and-response speaking exercises, which is really only helpful to the top 5-10% of students--those students answer nearly immediately and the remaining 90-95% of students has the answer given to them and doesn't have a chance to think through the problem on their own, which decreases how much they're learning in the session. I've noticed a significant decrease in my speaking facility (and an increase in how much I have to study) since we've transitioned away from group-wise work.
  • Three days of lecture, 2 days of drills. Yes, it was very productive since it balanced new information with practice.
  • Format was not productive. There was hardly any "drill" or oral repetition in class. Spent too much time watching the sitcoms, which actually had very little speaking in them. But most of all, time was wasted on the peanut gallery in class. Instructor and about 5 students distracted everyone with pointless repartee, which at times took up 40-50% of the class. Instructor also spent more time discussing exceptions rather than grammatical rules because students were always trying to ask clever questions. By the end of the quarter, I, and many other students, struggled to remember basic vocab and grammar rules, even though this class had been meeting every day. It wasn't that there was too much material, but the time allotted to learning that material was not well spent.
  • Lecture followed by partner exercises
  • Lecture three times a week and drills twice. Allowed for a day in between learning new material to become comfortable with it.
  • Lecture M, W, F Drill T, R
  • Lecture m/w/f Drills T/R This format worked very well. The drills reinforced the previously learned material, and occasionally introduced new material.
  • The format works well. It's nice to go over the grammar in the smaller drill sections.

How would you improve this course?

  • There are reading assignments on the syllabus! When I was in Bronwyn's section last quarter, I did the reading, as did about half of the class I think. In this section, almost nobody does the assigned reading (myself included). I think it does really help make lecture more effective both individually and for the class as a whole. When introducing a topic, Bronwyn would often ask people to describe what they knew about it (which typically came from their understanding of the readings)--these explanations were not perfect, but Bronwyn expanded on them and generalized them. It encouraged students to do the reading and it really helped to keep the class as a whole more engaged and on the same page with the presentation of material (and, consequently, more prepared to break out into active practice afterwards). Additionally, as I mentioned previously, I think really making it a priority to include groupwise speaking exercises instead of class-wide speaking exercises is key. We have drifted towards class-wide exercises in the past few weeks and I can tell I am not learning the material as effectively. Lastly, I think prompt returning of homework, quizzes, and exams is really key in this course. Since homework is due so frequently, I find myself making the same mistakes over and over again because I haven't gotten the feedback on my assignments yet. By the time I get it, I've often trained myself to make these mistakes and it's harder to unlearn them. It makes studying for exams and quizzes much more stressful, especially since lately we've been getting all the homework back the day or two before the exam.
  • Nothing
  • More repetition, change the assignments so that they reinforce grammar rules and vocabulary more systematically. I can't be "creative" until I know the mechanics. Needs to emphasize speaking and reading a lot more. Also, cap the class at 12 students because our class was way too big and we never had a room that could accommodate everyone.
